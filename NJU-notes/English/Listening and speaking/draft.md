Workshop Draft: The Danger of a Single Narrative

Title: The Story You Didn’t Hear

Learning Objective:

• Understand what a single story is and how it creates bias.

• Recognize the dangers of judging with incomplete information.

• Learn to seek multiple perspectives to build empathy and avoid stereotypes.

Key Words

1.Narrative（叙事）:

“Our stories, opinions, and descriptions about a person, event, or group.”

我们对一个人、一件事或一个群体的「故事、看法、描述」。

2.Single Narrative（单一叙事）

“Defining a person or event using only one perspective, one piece of information, or one label, without considering the whole picture or listening to other voices.”

只用一个角度、一部分信息、一个标签去定义完整的人或事，不看全貌，不听其他声音。

Opening: 3 minutes

Open from common daily instances (something interesting and easy to understand).

E.g.：“A student with a short haircut and a thermos should not get a low math score?”

保温杯寸头数学不该低分？

Invite participants to share their opinions and discuss. （1.5分钟）

Prepare examples in the PPT to supplement or prevent awkward pauses.

Point out the danger: “The danger of a single story: it is not that we intend to be bad; it is that the less information we have, the more likely we are to be biased. Seeing only one side, we misjudge or hurt others, and we ourselves are also misunderstood.”

提出单一叙事的危害：不是我们故意坏，而是信息越少，我们越容易偏见；只看一面，我们就会误解、伤害别人，也被别人误解。（1分钟）

Activity 1 – Story Telephone / 单一故事传话游戏 – 6 分钟

目的 / Objective

• 让学生亲身体验 信息片面 → 偏见形成 → 完整故事改变判断

• 强调 One Story 永远不够

所需材料 / Materials

• 纸条或小卡片，每张写一段完整故事

Option 1: Talked Back to the Teacher

Jamie disagreed with the teacher in class. She raised her hand and spoke politely because she had a different idea from the textbook. The teacher later thanked her for sharing another perspective.

Option 2: Late for Class

Alex was late for class this morning.He missed the bus because he helped an old woman who fell down on the street.Usually, Alex is responsible and never late.

Option 3: Group Project

Taylor did not finish the slides on time.Her computer broke, so she stayed up late rewriting the notes on her phone.She was quiet in the meeting because she felt nervous and did not want to make excuses.

• PPT 用于展示总结概念

活动步骤 / Steps

Step 1: 准备故事（1 min）

1. 准备好完整故事，选两组，每组三人。

2. 将故事传给第一名学生 只读一次。

• 学生简短地概括故事内容，尽量快速传递。

Step 2: 传话游戏（2 min）

1. 学生排成一列，第一位学生用自己的话 向下一位学生口述

• 只能用一部分信息，尽量自然

• 不允许看到纸条

2. 下一位学生接收后再传给下一位，依次传到最后一个学生

3. 目标：每次传递都可能丢失信息或产生偏差 → 学生自然形成偏见

Step 3: 揭示完整故事（1 min）

• 展示原始完整故事

• 对比最后传到的版本和原故事

• 强调：片面信息导致的误解和偏差

Step 4: 总结+承上启下（1min）：

• “When we only hear part of a story, we can form biased judgments. One story is never enough.”

• 中文：信息不完整会悄悄形成偏见，一个故事永远不够

Activity 2: Cross-Cultural Homestay Role Play (单一视角情景表演) – 7 minutes

Scenario: Chinese Family Hosting an International Student with a Bottle of Wine

背景 / Context:

• 中国学生邀请国际交流生到家里吃晚饭（homestay）。

• 交流生带来一瓶酒作为礼物。

• 角色 / Roles:

1. Chinese student (host)

2. International student (guest)

3. Host parents

4. Audience / observers

Role Play Flow / 表演流程 可以把内心独白表现出来

0.熟悉剧情（1min）

1. Host parents show hospitality (1.5 min)

父母准备丰盛晚餐，同时注意到交流生带来酒作为礼物

台词示例：

• 父母（Parents）：“Try more! This is our special dish.”

多吃点，这是我们家最拿手的菜。

• 交流生（Guest）：“Thank you, I’m full. Also, I brought this wine for you.”

谢谢，我已经吃饱了。另外，我带了一瓶酒给你们。

2. Cross-cultural misunderstanding arises (Single Story)(2.5 min)

• 交流生内心想：“They keep insisting on more food. Why don’t they seem to notice my gift? Are they being pushy?”

为什么他们一直坚持加菜，却对我带的礼物没反应？他们太强势了吗？

• 家长内心想：“He brought alcohol? That’s unusual! And he’s refusing our food? He doesn’t appreciate our hospitality at all!”

他竟然带酒？这不太寻常！还拒绝我们的菜？完全不懂礼貌！

Summary / Takeaway (1 min)

引导：文化差异导致不同解读 → 单一故事造成误解

• 强调核心概念：

“One story is never enough. Different cultural perspectives can lead to different interpretations. Understanding multiple viewpoints prevents bias and misunderstandings.”

一个故事永远不够，每个人的视角只是全貌的一部分。理解多种视角可以避免偏见和误解。

• 交流生只看到父母坚持加菜 → 觉得父母强势

• 家长只看到交流生拒绝菜 + 带酒 → 觉得学生不懂礼貌

• 每个人只听到/看到“一部分故事” → 产生偏见

• 完整故事 / 多角度理解 → 改变判断，减少误解

Notes / Tips

• 道具：餐具、小礼物（酒瓶可用道具或图片）

• 台词口语化自然

• 强调体验 单一故事导致偏见，而不是表演完美

Wrap-up – 4 minutes

Key Summary:（1分钟）

1. “Single narrative equals to seeing only one side, hearing only one sentence, using only one label. Its most dangerous aspect is that it quietly creates bias without us even noticing.”

单一叙事 = 只看一面、只听一句、只用一个标签。它最可怕的地方，是悄悄制造偏见，我们自己都察觉不到。

2. Dangers:（1.5分钟）

• Creates unfair stereotypes and prejudice（产生偏见和刻板印象）

• Makes us judge people too quickly, without knowing the whole truth（草率评判）

• Divides people and blocks understanding（制造隔阂）

• Limits our thinking—we only see one side, and lose the whole picture（局限思维）

Core Reminder:（1.5分钟）

• “Everyone has multiple sides, every event has multiple perspectives, and every culture has many stories.”

每个人都有很多面，每件事有多个角度，每种文化都有很多故事。

文化差异导致不同解读 → 单一故事造成误解

• “Do not let a single impression block the full truth.”

不要让单一印象挡住完整真相。

Before you judge, remember: Always look for more than one story.
